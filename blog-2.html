Unlimited TBHI Training

TBHI (Training for Basic Health Improvement) programs focus on educating individuals and healthcare workers about preventive healthcare practices. These trainings cover essential topics such as hygiene awareness, early disease detection, nutrition guidance, and community health management.

What Is TBHI?

Training for Basic Health Improvement (TBHI) is a structured educational program designed to bridge the gap between general awareness and actionable health practices. It targets both healthcare professionals and the general public.

Core Training Modules

  • Hygiene Awareness: Hand hygiene, sanitation protocols, and infection prevention techniques.
  • Early Disease Detection: Recognizing common symptoms of fever, malaria, typhoid, and other illnesses early.
  • Nutrition Guidance: Balanced diet planning for different age groups and health conditions.
  • Community Health Management: Organizing health camps, awareness drives, and vaccination programs.
  • First Aid: Basic first-aid procedures for common emergencies.

Who Should Attend?

TBHI training is ideal for community health workers, school teachers, NGO volunteers, and anyone who wants to actively contribute to improving health outcomes in their community. No prior medical knowledge is required to begin the foundational courses.

Benefits of TBHI

By participating in TBHI training, individuals gain the knowledge and skills required to promote healthier lifestyles and improve public health outcomes. Communities with trained health volunteers show significantly lower rates of preventable diseases.
