Medicine Safety Tips

Important guidelines every patient should know

Never Self-Medicate

Taking medicines without a prescription — especially antibiotics — can cause antibiotic resistance and serious side effects.

Check Expiry Dates

Always check the expiry date before consuming any medicine. Expired medicines can be ineffective or harmful.

Store Properly

Store medicines in a cool, dry place away from sunlight. Some medications (like insulin) require refrigeration.

Keep Away from Children

Always store medicines out of reach of children. Use child-proof caps and lock medicine cabinets where possible.

Complete the Full Course

Always complete the full prescribed course even if you feel better early — stopping antibiotics midway causes resistance.

Report Allergic Reactions

If you experience rash, difficulty breathing, or swelling after taking a medicine — stop immediately and call a doctor.
